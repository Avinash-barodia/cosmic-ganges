What Is This?

Reiki (pronounced Ray-Key) is a Japanese technique for stress reduction, relaxation, and energetic healing. It is based on the understanding that an unseen "life force energy" flows through us and causes us to be alive. When this energy is low or blocked due to stress, trauma, or illness, we are more likely to feel unwell physically or emotionally.

During a Reiki session, this life force energy is channeled into your energetic field, clearing blockages and restoring harmony. It treats the whole person—including body, emotions, mind, and spirit—creating many beneficial effects that include relaxation and feelings of peace, security, and wellbeing.

Distance Reiki sessions are just as effective as in-person sessions, as energy transcends time and space. At the scheduled time, you will be asked to lie down in a quiet, comfortable space where you will not be disturbed. We may begin with a brief call to set intentions.

As the Reiki energy is channeled to you, you may feel sensations of warmth, tingling, or a deep, comforting heaviness. Many clients drift into a deeply restful sleep. The energy automatically flows to where it is needed most, clearing heavy energies and restoring your natural vitality.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does distance Reiki work?

In quantum physics, everything is connected through an energetic field. Reiki practitioners are trained to tune into your specific energetic frequency, allowing them to send healing energy across any distance with the same effectiveness as an in-person session.

What will I feel during the session?

Experiences vary. You might feel heat, tingling, or pulsing sensations. You may see colors or experience emotional releases. Or, you may just feel incredibly relaxed and fall asleep. All experiences are valid and the energy is working regardless of physical sensation.

Are there any side effects?

Reiki is completely safe. Occasionally, as toxins and heavy energies leave your system, you may experience a brief "healing crisis"—feeling slightly tired, emotional, or needing more water for a day or two as the body recalibrates.
